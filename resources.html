Upcoming Events

What is IIIF? Having Fun with IIIF and Japanese Visual Materials

October 11, 2018

Register

This hands-on, interactive workshop, supported by the Reischauer Institute of Japanese Studies and led by members of DARTH (Arts & Humanities Research Computing) and the the Japan Digital Research Center (JDRC) will introduce participants to the International Image Interoperability Framework (IIIF) and open-source IIIF image viewer Mirador. We will explore how to find IIIF resources, especially Japanese-language visual materials; learn how to use Mirador; and learn what goes into a IIIF manifest.

Registration requested by October 5.

Past Events

IIIF New England Meetup

March 14-15, 2018

This two-day event, co-hosted by Harvard and MIT and held at the Barker Center and the Harvard University Art Museums, brought together developers, implementers, and other adopters to explain the background to IIIF, its growing community, and to demonstrate the potential of the Framework and the innovative ways in which it can be used to present digital image collections. The Showcase event included presentations from Boston Public Library, Brown University, Colby College, Harvard University, MIT, Princeton University, Yale University, and others. The Technical Workshop, structed as an unconference, provided opportunities to discuss implementations, issues, initiatives and developments in IIIF.

Hacking Mirador: Building Scholarly Scrapbooks

October 7, 2016

The goal of this workshop, the first of four in the DARTH Fall 2016 Lunch & Learn series, was to introduce participants to the International Image Interoperability Framework (IIIF) and the open-source IIIF image viewer Mirador. A walk-through tutorial is available here.
